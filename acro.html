About Acro

Acro is a general term for all activities that involve some sort of acrobatic movement but most of the time it means AcroYoga and Partner Acrobatics.

AcroYoga is a physical practice which combines yoga and acrobatics. AcroYoga may provide physical and mental health benefits. In addition to the exercise and strength building aspects of AcroYoga the partner balancing can improve concentration and the massage elements can provide stress relief. However Acroyoga is more vigorous than many traditional yoga practices and this may lead to more injuries.

Partner Acrobatics or Acrobalance is an acrobatic art that combines elements of adagio and hand balancing. Adagio consists of partner lifts, where one person lifts another in many different poses and positions. Many forms of adagio also incorporate throws and tosses.

Hand balancing is the performance of acrobatic body shape changing movements, or stationary poses, or both, while balanced on and supported entirely by one's hands or arms. It is performed by acro dancers, circus performers, gymnasts, and sports acrobats. Acrobalance acts require a high degree of care, coordination, proprioceptive awareness, and mutual trust from the performers in order to avoid injury.

There are three primary roles in an Acro practice: base, flyer, and spotter. Click on the image to the right to enlarge it.

  • Base - this is the individual who has the most points of contact with the ground. Often this person is lying on the ground with the entire back torso in full contact. This enables both the arms and legs to be "bone-stacked" for maximum stability and support of the Flyer. Main points of contact with the flyer are the feet (generally placed on the Flyer's hips, groin or lower abdomen) and the hands (which either form handholds or grasp the shoulders).
  • Flyer - this is the individual who is elevated off the ground by the Base. The Flyer can move into a series of dynamic positions, and generally lets gravity do the work for them. A Flyer needs balance, confidence, and core strength.
  • Spotter - this is the individual who has an objective view of the partners, and whose entire focus is on making sure that the Flyer lands safely in case of any slips. The spotter can also make recommendations to the Base and Flyer to improve their form.

In AcroYoga base is mostly laying on the ground also referred to as L-basing, while in Acrobalance or Partner Acrobatics base is usually in the standing position.

People in these communities often refer to eachother as monkeys and doing acro as playing. They also come from all sorts of different backgrounds besides acro. To mention a few: aerial and trapeze arts, slacklining, gumnastics, circus, cyr wheel, chinese pole, juggling, dance, parkour, hoops, capoeira etc.

There are gatherings in local parks all over the world almost everywhere and every day... If you see pople doing handstands (all acro people are obsessed with handstands) or you see people balancing on each other come over and ask if you can play... Nobody will say 'no'...

Other terms you'll learn if you decide to give Acro a try are: pop, whip, pitch, cradle, stradle, pike, washing machine etc.

Where to Learn Acro

AcroFinder is an app for the Acroyoga, slacklining, yoga and circus communities and more! It lets you find acro classes and jams around you whenever you want to practice. You can organize jams in just a few taps, RSVP to let people know you will be joining and share your current location to allow other practitioners to find you easily. AcroFinder makes it easy and intuitive to get together with your community to train, play and meet up.

If you are looking for a class, AcroFinder leverages an integration with AcroMaps Website that allows you to find classes near you or any location. The list of classes is ordered by date starting from today, making it fast and easy to identify what classes are near you right now!
